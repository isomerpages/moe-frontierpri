<p>At Frontier Primary School, we are committed to fostering a safe, respectful,
and inclusive environment where every Frontierer feels a strong sense of
belonging. We believe that every child has the right to learn and grow.</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; We take a firm and proactive stance against
all forms of bullying and hurtful behaviour. This stance guides our school
in managing different forms of hurtful behaviours.</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; We recognise that:</p>
<p>a.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; students may have disagreements or say things
they do not mean.</p>
<p>b.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; the school will help students understand the
difference between occasional conflicts (which are normal) and persistent
bullying behaviour, addressing each situation with care and appropriate
support.</p>
<p>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; We need the support from parents to nurture
empathy, strengthen relationships, and guide our students to make positive
choices that reflect responsibility, respect and care for others.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Enabling a caring and supportive environment for all students</strong>
</p>
<p>All staff of Frontier Primary School seek to:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; foster the class and school
environment, with positive teacher-student relationships and strong peer
support structure with trained student leaders and peer support leaders</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; conduct Character &amp; Citizenship
Education lessons on empathy, conflict management, resilience and cyber
wellness</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; conduct regular chitchat sessions
with students (Heart-to-Heart Talk)</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; take a firm stance against
use of unkind words and hurtful behaviours</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Support students in reporting Bullying</strong>
</p>
<p>Students and /or parents can reach out to the school in the following
ways:</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Direct reporting to any staff member in school,
for example, Form Teachers, Subject Teachers and Year Head</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Students can drop a written message into the
letter box outside Counselling Room at Level 2. The messages are retrieved
only by the School Counsellor, Ms Joyce Siew.</p>

<p><strong><u>Definition of Bullying</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Bullying involves persistent and intentional hurtful acts, while other
hurtful behaviours include once-off insensitive comments and physical fights.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Bullying and hurtful behaviours can take various forms, including verbal,
social, physical and cyber:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Verbal: Name-calling, spreading
rumours, or making threats</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Social exclusion: Deliberately
leaving someone out, or encouraging others to ignore someone</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Physical actions: Pushing,
hitting, or damaging someone's belongings</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Cyber bullying: Using online
technology like phones, computers, or social media to hurt or embarrass
others on purpose and repeatedly</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Case Management will only be initiated once we have ascertained that the
incident constitutes a bullying case. For behaviours that do not constitute
bullying, we will still address them appropriately through other means
of support and intervention.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Bullying - Case Management</strong>
</p>
<p>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Investigation Process and Timeline</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; Upon being alerted to the incident/offence, the school will
inform parents about the incident/offence (within the same day)&nbsp;and
seek&nbsp;parents' support&nbsp;to&nbsp;monitor student well-being&nbsp;and
safety, and ensure the student is well-supported at home.</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school will seek the support from parents to keep channels
of communication open with school personnel.</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school will appoint a main point-of-contact for communications
with parents.&nbsp;</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; In terms of investigation and updates, as broad timeline
for reference,&nbsp;the school will adhere to Service Standards in the
Civil Service (within the day, 3-7-21 days) for communications with parents.</p>
<p>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Activation of Care Plan for students</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school will kickstart mandatory counselling for students
when necessary. &nbsp;</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school speak to students and to draw up a Care Plan.</p>
<p>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Establishing Partnership</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school will work with parents of students involved and
to inform parents the outcomes of the investigation at the earliest opportunity.</p>
<p>4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Consequences</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school takes reference from MOE Baseline Consequence
as seen at Table 1 and draws up consequences.</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; The school will mandate counselling for students involved
and observe readiness of students before starting the restoration of relationships
and learning prosocial skills. For cases which require more support for
students involved, the school will draw on external referrals and community
resources.</p>

<p><strong>Respect for self and others (the Frontierer Identity)</strong>
</p>
<p>Frontier Primary School takes a firm stance against all forms of bullying
and hurtful behaviours, as they reflect a disregard for self-respect and
respect for others. We seek parents’ support in partnering with us to guide
our students in upholding these values when such behaviours occur. We hope
that all parents can;</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; work closely with staff to support their child in understanding
the impact of their actions.</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; support staff when staff provide appropriate support for
students involved, which may include:
<br>a. Counselling and guidance to develop self-respect, empathy, and respect
for others;
<br>b. Restorative conversations and interventions to repair relationships
where appropriate;</p>
<p>o&nbsp;&nbsp; support the school when the school metes out appropriate
baseline school-based disciplinary consequences. Bullying and hurtful behaviours
are taken<strong> seriously, with measures calibrated based on the nature and severity of the case.</strong>
</p>
